**What changed April -> July, in one sentence:** the `sm_121a` build fix (#37725, late March) made the hardware E2M1 path correct, FlashInfer's April-May SM12x campaign (#3013 -> #3026/#3051/#3066/#3152) made the CUTLASS FP4 GEMM/fused-MoE fast, and vLLM #40082 (May 20) wired the b12x/cutedsl variants — so by the 26.05.post1 container the auto-selector legitimately picks `FLASHINFER_CUTLASS` NvFp4 MoE + `FlashInferCutlassNvFp4LinearKernel` instead of Marlin. Your observed backend list (FLASHINFER_TRTLLM / CUTEDSL / CUTEDSL_BATCHED / CUTLASS / VLLM_CUTLASS / MARLIN / EMULATION) matches the post-#40082 enum; FLASHINFER_TRTLLM stays SM100-only per the audit.

**Unresolved/caveats (verified):** `flashinfer-cubin` 0.6.11 ships zero sm_120/121 cubins (all Sm100a/f/103a), so SM121 relies on JIT/`120f` wheel paths ([flashinfer#3294](https://github.com/flashinfer-ai/flashinfer/issues/3294)); a community report claims +78% from building FlashInfer with native SM12.1 vs SM12.0 forward-compat (community-reported, single source). On the container version: NVIDIA's notes list 26.05 = vLLM **0.20.1** and never mention a `26.05.post1` tag or 0.21.0-NV (verified absence) — your observed `0.21.0-NV` in `26.05.post1-py3` is user-observed primary evidence that the post1 respin bumped vLLM; treat the exact upstream diff as **unverified**.

## 2. Your 63-67 tok/s vs community 97-120 tok/s

**Verified: the 97-120 figures are (a) a different model with a native MTP head, and (b) MTP speculative decoding and/or a non-vLLM engine.**

- The headline figures come from **Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-NVFP4** (successor MoE, ~3B active, **ships a native MTP head**), not Qwen3-30B-A3B. Measured on DGX Spark, `vllm/vllm-openai:v0.25.0-aarch64`: **baseline no-spec-decode 76.66 tok/s** (NVIDIA quant; 67.52 Unsloth quant); **MTP-2 = 97.96; MTP-3 = 108.30 tok/s** ([classmethod.jp benchmark](https://dev.classmethod.jp/en/articles/dgx-spark-qwen3-6-35b-a3b-nvfp4-new-champion/)). The vlaicu playbook independently reports **102 tok/s with MTP-3** (125 in thinking mode, higher acceptance) ([vlaicu.io](https://vlaicu.io/posts/dgx-vllm/)). The [llmrequirements 97 tok/s single-stream](https://llmrequirements.com/news/2026-06-03-nvfp4-qwen-3-6-35b-dgx-spark) config is described as FlashInfer attention + CUTLASS-FP4 MoE + **MTP-3**. The **120 tok/s** figure traces to "Atlas," a custom pure-Rust engine, not vLLM ([tesseraai](https://tesseraai.cloud/en/blog/qwen-3-6-35b-dgx-spark-tokens-per-second/)). One source (tesseraai) implies 97 tok/s "without spec decode" — refuted 2:1 by classmethod's explicit 76.66 baseline and llmrequirements' MTP-3 config; treat as conflation.
- **Typical WITHOUT spec decode on GB10 for ~3-3.3B-active NVFP4 MoE (verified data points):** 55.9 tok/s (Qwen3.6-35B, April cu130-nightly, `--moe-backend=flashinfer_cutlass`, [stevescargall](https://stevescargall.com/blog/2026/04/vllm-recipe-redhatai/qwen3.6-35b-a3b-nvfp4-on-dgx-spark/) — though MTP flag was on, acceptance-adjusted); 50-56 tok/s no-draft for Gemma-4-26B-A4B-NVFP4 / Nemotron-3-Nano-30B-A3B ([vlaicu.io](https://vlaicu.io/posts/dgx-vllm/)); 32-50 tok/s Qwen3-30B-A3B on the older 26.04 stack ([conselara](https://conselara.dev/notes/dgx-spark-model-selection/)); 67.5-76.7 tok/s Qwen3.6-35B on v0.25.0.
- **Inferred verdict:** your 63-67 tok/s (TPOT 14.8ms) for Qwen3-30B-A3B on the 26.05.post1/0.21.0-NV stack is squarely in — slightly above the middle of — the no-spec-decode band for this class, and there is no evidence anyone reaches 97+ on vLLM/GB10 for a ~3B-active MoE without speculative decoding. Your ~145-175 tok/s at c=4 also tracks the published concurrency scaling (322 tok/s aggregate at c=8 for the 35B, with spec decode).

## 3. Bandwidth roofline check (arithmetic; **inferred from verified architecture specs**)

Qwen3-30B-A3B: 48 layers, hidden 2048, 32Q/4KV heads (head_dim 128), 128 experts top-8, expert FFN 768, vocab 151,936. Weights read per decode token:

- Attention: 48 x (2048x4096 + 4096x2048 + 2x2048x512) ≈ **0.91B params**
- Active experts: 48 x 8 x 3 x (2048x768) ≈ **1.81B**; routers ≈ 0.013B
- lm_head: 151,936 x 2048 ≈ **0.31B** (modelopt NVFP4 checkpoints typically leave lm_head in BF16)

NVFP4 = 4-bit weight + FP8 scale per 16-block = 4.5 bits = **0.5625 B/param**. So: quantized (0.91+1.81+0.01) x 0.5625 ≈ **1.54 GB** + lm_head in BF16 0.62 GB ≈ **2.16 GB/token** (1.85 GB if lm_head is FP8). KV reads at 1K context: 48 x 2 x 4 x 128 x 2B ≈ 96 KB per context-token ≈ **0.1 GB/step** — second-order.

- Absolute ceiling at 273 GB/s: 273 / 2.16 ≈ **126 tok/s** (148 if FP8 head).
- Realistic sustained LPDDR5x bandwidth on GB10 is ~210-230 GB/s (~80%): weight-read floor ≈ 9.4-10.3 ms/token -> **~97-106 tok/s** before any compute/overhead.
- Your 14.8 ms TPOT = 9.4-10.3 ms memory floor + ~4.5-5.4 ms of attention/activation traffic, kernel launches, MoE dispatch, sampling, and CPU-side step overhead -> **~60-65% of the practical roofline**.

**Conclusion (inferred, arithmetic-backed): 63-67 tok/s without spec decode is exactly what the roofline predicts for a well-functioning bandwidth-bound stack; you are not sitting on a hidden 2x from kernels alone.** The remaining single-stream headroom is (a) shaving the ~5ms/step non-GEMM overhead (async scheduling, full CUDA graphs) — maybe +10-20% — and (b) speculative decoding, which is how everyone gets past ~80.

## 4. Best-known extra speedups on GB10 in this container class

**Verified:**
- **MoE backend choice barely matters anymore on SM121:** b12x/cutedsl vs flashinfer_cutlass = +1.8%/+6.0% ([PR #40082](https://github.com/vllm-project/vllm/pull/40082)); Marlin's 16% edge was a March-era artifact ([forum #365119](https://forums.developer.nvidia.com/t/marlin-fix-nvfp4-actually-works-on-sm121-dgx-spark/365119)); v0.25.0 auto-select even picks MARLIN and still hits 76.7 tok/s ([classmethod](https://dev.classmethod.jp/en/articles/dgx-spark-qwen3-6-35b-a3b-nvfp4-new-champion/)); the vLLM DGX Spark blog recommends leaving selection on auto ([vllm.ai blog](https://vllm.ai/blog/2026-06-01-vllm-dgx-spark)). Worth an A/B, expect single digits.
- **CUDA graphs: keep on.** `--enforce-eager` costs ~55% ([conselara](https://conselara.dev/notes/dgx-spark-model-selection/)). Known SM121 graph-capture crash affects only the TRTLLM FP4 MoE path ([flashinfer#2776](https://github.com/flashinfer-ai/flashinfer/issues/2776)), not your CUTLASS path.
- **`--async-scheduling`** recommended for MoE/hybrid models; `--enable-prefix-caching`; `--max-num-seqs` 1-4; `--gpu-memory-utilization` 0.7-0.85 on unified memory ([vlaicu.io](https://vlaicu.io/posts/dgx-vllm/), [NVIDIA 26.05 notes](https://docs.nvidia.com/deeplearning/frameworks/vllm-release-notes/rel-26-05.html)).
- **fastsafetensors** (`--load-format fastsafetensors`): cold-start load time only (10-15 min -> less), zero effect on decode throughput ([vlaicu.io](https://vlaicu.io/posts/dgx-vllm/)).
- **fp8 KV cache:** mixed evidence — used in the fastest published Spark benches, but the vLLM blog measured a "noticeable performance cost" on Spark; A/B it ([vllm.ai blog](https://vllm.ai/blog/2026-06-01-vllm-dgx-spark)).
- **Spec decode for BASE Qwen3-30B-A3B (no MTP head):** `method:"mtp"` is unavailable — the checkpoint has no MTP weights. vLLM 0.21 options: **EAGLE3 draft** — [AngelSlim/Qwen3-a3B_eagle3](https://huggingface.co/AngelSlim/Qwen3-a3B_eagle3) exists for Qwen3-30B-A3B (accept length 1.8-3.5, 1.4-1.9x measured at spec 2-4 on H20); **ngram/suffix decoding** (`method:"ngram"`, free, only helps echo-heavy workloads); classic **draft-model**; or [Speculators](https://github.com/vllm-project/speculators)-trained heads.

**Inferred:** on GB10, an EAGLE3 draft should net roughly 1.3-1.6x single-stream (drafting overhead is proportionally larger on a bandwidth-starved chip than on H20, and drafts run BF16); the single biggest lever the 97-120 crowd actually used is a **model swap to Qwen3.6-35B-A3B with its native MTP head** (~1.4x at MTP-3 over its own 76.7 baseline). Community DFlash (2.2-2.7x) requires vLLM >=0.21 plus a patched sm121 build and reportedly loses acceptance on NVFP4 targets — experimental. Disable spec decode above ~batch 8-32 (`--speculative-disable-by-batch-size`).
